Security

Blockchain allows for a much closer look at how important information is stored. By storing information that cannot be changed after it is stored and by the extensive encryption provided, your data is safe and undeniable. It can be determined who gets access to the data.

In its most common form, data in the blockchain is stored in blocks, with each new block connected to the last block. This is done in a way that makes it virtually impossible thereafter to change the information stored in a previous block. All blocks of information can be added to the chain if there is a consensus across the network, how this consensus is determined is defined in a pre-agreed protocol.

Automation

Transactions can be automated with smart contracts that increase efficiency and speed up the process even more. Once pre-specified conditions are met, the next step in the transaction or process is automatically initiated. Smart contracts reduce human intervention and reliance on third parties to verify that the terms of a contract have been met. In insurance, for example, a claim can be automatically settled and paid once a customer has provided all the necessary documentation to file a claim.

This automation is where our project is focused on. Machines can check whether certain actions, such as drilling or welding on a particular product, have been performed correctly. Once it has been verified that the required steps have been performed, a machine can determine whether to perform a follow-up operation on a product.

Transparancy

Blockchain creates an audit trail that documents the origin of a product at every step of its journey. In industries where consumers are concerned about environmental or human rights issues surrounding a product - or an industry that suffers from counterfeiting and fraud - this helps provide proof. With blockchain, it is possible to share provenance data directly with customers. Traceability data can also reveal weaknesses in a supply chain - where goods may be sitting on a loading dock awaiting transit.

Efficiency

Traditional paper-heavy processes are time-consuming, prone to human error and often require third-party mediation. By streamlining these processes with blockchain, transactions can be completed faster and more efficiently. Documentation can be stored on the blockchain along with transaction details, eliminating the need to exchange paper data. There is no need to reconcile multiple databases, allowing for much faster approval and processing.
